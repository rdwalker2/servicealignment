What Your Current Process Is Missing

What we heard from Complete Fence about the current recruiting workflow

01

Paycom's ATS Is Unusable

This isn't just "we wish it were better." Paycom is actively getting in the way. Hiring managers can't navigate it. Candidate views are buried under layers of clicks. If a manager wasn't trained on day one, they're lost. Even finding applicants on an open req is harder than it should be. Every step creates friction, and that friction is costing the team time and candidates.

02

Candidate Experience Is Uncontrolled

Complete Fence has been clear that this is the biggest frustration. Right now there's no clean application flow, no automated acknowledgements, and no control over communication. Candidates might get an abrupt auto-rejection with zero personalization, and the team can't even delay those emails, customize them, or see what's being sent. For a company that cares about its reputation, that matters. It directly affects who applies and whether good candidates stick around.

03

No Employer Brand / Career Site

Complete Fence has a great story: family-owned since '76, veteran-owned, #51 on Inc. Regionals, but candidates never see any of that. There's no career site, no place to showcase culture, and no way for TA to own the employer brand without waiting on marketing (which doesn't have a big team right now anyway). Candidates find you on job boards and have zero reason to pick Complete Fence over the next posting.

04

Zero Reporting or Executive Visibility

Complete Fence leadership is analytical. They want to see data, not just hear that "hiring is going fine." Time to hire, source tracking, pipeline conversion, ad spend ROI. None of that exists today. Everything is gut feel and manual Excel tracking. If leadership asks "where are our best candidates coming from?" or "what's our cost per hire?" right now, there's no good answer.

"Leadership is analytical and will care strongly about both cost and visibility."
— On what matters for executive approval
05

Hiring Managers Don't Want to Be in the System

Complete Fence's hiring managers are out running crews and projects. They don't want to learn another platform, and they shouldn't have to. They need to see their roles, review candidates, and give feedback — and that's it. If the system is too clicky or confusing, they'll just stop using it. That's exactly what happened with Paycom, and it means HR ends up doing everyone's job on top of their own.

06

No Tracked Referral Process

In the trades, referrals are gold, but right now yours come in through email or word of mouth with no tracking, no attribution, and no way to know who referred whom or what happened with the candidate. You can't build a referral culture if nobody can see whether their referrals actually got hired.

07

High-Volume Unqualified Applicants

Complete Fence flagged this specifically: when office or remote roles go up, the team gets flooded with unqualified applicants. There's no AI screening, no smart filtering, and no way to separate signal from noise at scale. Every single application has to be reviewed by hand, and for a one-person TA function, that's hours lost on people who were never going to be a fit.

08

Two Business Entities, One Recruiting Need

Complete Fence and Northern Illinois are different worlds when it comes to hiring. Complete Fence is more office-oriented. Northern Illinois is field-based and unionized. People come and go more frequently, and some installers bypass the normal recruiting process entirely (they just show up and get onboarded). Whatever ATS you choose has to handle both realities, or it's only solving half the problem.

5. "Is there a sandbox or test environment we can validate against?" — So we can test the data flow before go-live without affecting production payroll.

How It Actually Works

Step-by-step playbooks for each non-negotiable

Saying "yes, it works" isn't enough. Here's exactly how each workflow runs, step by step, so there's no ambiguity about what happens, who does what, and where the data ends up.

Playbook 1

Candidate Gets Hired: How Data Flows to Lumber Payroll

1
The recruiter drags the candidate card to the "Hired" stage
This is a single drag-and-drop action on the pipeline board. Same as moving any candidate between stages.
2
Teamtailor automatically fires a webhook
Behind the scenes, a pre-configured trigger sends all the candidate's data to an endpoint. Name, email, phone, resume, and any custom fields (start date, pay rate, cost code, etc.).
3
A middleware layer (Zapier or custom) formats the data for Lumber
This is a simple data mapping step. Teamtailor says "first_name," Lumber might say "employee_first_name." The middleware translates between the two. Takes about 30 minutes to configure, then it runs without intervention.
4
Lumber receives the new hire and creates the employee record
The person shows up in Lumber ready for payroll setup. No re-keying the data, no copy-pasting from one system to the other.
What the recruiter sees:
They drag a card. That's it. Everything after that happens automatically. No second system to log into, no data entry, no export/import.
What still needs to happen before go-live:
One technical call with Lumber to confirm their API field names and authentication method for receiving new hire data from Teamtailor. We recommend doing this before making a final decision — see the "What to Ask Lumber" checklist below.
Playbook 2

E-Verify and I-9: How It Works

I-9 and E-Verify are managed in the E-Verify portal — that doesn't change. Teamtailor's role is to make sure the step doesn't get missed.

1
Candidate is marked "Hired" in Teamtailor
2
TT Onboarding kicks in: new hire gets a welcome email with their task checklist
Sign safety docs, watch orientation video, confirm emergency contacts, complete any company-specific paperwork.
3
A reminder task is assigned to HR: "Complete E-Verify for [Name]"
This is a tracked task in the onboarding checklist with a configurable deadline and reminders. It ensures the E-Verify step doesn't fall through the cracks.
4
HR handles I-9 and E-Verify in the E-Verify portal (same as today)
This is a manual step — ~5 minutes per hire. The I-9 document verification and E-Verify submission happen in the government portal, not in Teamtailor.
5
HR marks the task complete in Teamtailor. Hire data flows to Lumber for payroll.
Key point: Teamtailor doesn't replace your E-Verify process — it just makes sure it doesn't get missed. The I-9 and E-Verify workflow stays in the government portal where it belongs. TT provides the reminder, the deadline tracking, and the audit trail.
