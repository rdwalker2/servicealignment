The "800 Million Candidate Profiles" — Where the Data Actually Comes From

ClearCompany's Sourcing Agent draws from a database of 800 million professional profiles. That number sounds massive, but it's worth understanding what's behind it.

Layer 1 · Public Web Data

LinkedIn profiles, GitHub, personal websites, social media — scraped and aggregated from the open internet.

Source: The internet
Layer 2 · Data Aggregators

Companies that clean, deduplicate, and structure this raw data into searchable APIs. They license it to recruiting tools.

People Data Labs (3B+), MixRank, Proxycurl
Layer 3 · Sourcing Products

Tools that license data from aggregators, add their own AI/search layer, and sell access — often per-credit.

ClearCo, Gem, Juicebox, hireEZ, SeekOut, Loxo

The "800 million" figure is an industry-standard number. Multiple competing sourcing tools cite the same scale because they're all drawing from the same underlying pool of publicly available professional data. ClearCompany has not disclosed their specific data provider, but the data itself is not proprietary to them.

The real question behind the sourcing decision

Your team is busy. You need the system to do the heavy lifting on sourcing — better enabling a lean recruitment team to find great candidates, especially passive talent who aren’t applying. That’s a real need. The question is: what’s the right way to solve it?

"The biggest piece is they have a sourcing agent. It's going out there... looking through all of that data and then highlighting the best picks. It's eliminating our need to even scrub through all those candidates. It's doing all of that within that AI agent. For me, that was pretty like, oh geez, that's really amazing."
— Your team, on why ClearCompany's Sourcing Agent was exciting

An agent that autonomously finds candidates sounds ideal for a small team. But it's worth asking: does autonomous sourcing on a cold database actually deliver for niche clinical research roles?

Approach A: Autonomous Agent on Cold Data

AI scans 800M profiles and surfaces "best matches." But those 800M profiles are strangers — they don't know Scout, haven't expressed interest, and may not respond. For niche roles (clinical monitors, regulatory specialists), keyword matching doesn't capture the qualifications that actually matter. You spend credits finding names, then spend time chasing people who may never reply.

Worth asking: request a live demo with a real Scout role. How many of the top 20 "matches" would you actually reach out to?

Approach B: Build a Sourcing Engine

Instead of chasing cold profiles, build a system that attracts candidates to you. Your career site captures passive interest (Connect). Nurture campaigns keep silver medalists warm for months. Copilot mines your growing database before you source externally. And when you need outbound reach, plug in best-of-breed sourcing tools via the open marketplace.

This is how a 2-person team builds capacity without adding headcount — and the pipeline compounds over time.
